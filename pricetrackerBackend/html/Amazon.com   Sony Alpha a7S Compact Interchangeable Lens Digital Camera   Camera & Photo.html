Review summary from DPReview

March 2015
Professional videographers and independent filmmakers, who may be a bit more deliberate in their choice of equipement and don't mind setting up a rig with an external recorder, will find a lot to like in this camera. For these users the a7S delivers an unquestionably cinematic look that's difficult to achieve without increasing your spend by an order of magnitude.
Read full review on DPReview (dpreview.com)

Personal takes

Dale BaskinReviewer
It's not surprising that the camera has started to develop a bit of a cult following among videographers. The a7S simply records beautiful video. Whether you shoot internally at 1080p or externally at 4K, the video files out of the a7S are among the very best we've seen from an interhchangable lens camera at this price point.
Rishi SanyalDeputy/Tech Editor
The Sony A7S faces stiff competition as a stills camera by solid DSLR offerings from its competitors, as well as from its own A7-series siblings, which generally offer better features for still imagery. But youll be hard-pressed to find a better video camera, where the A7S offers class-leading resolution and noise performance.

Reasons to buy

  • Video quality: The a7S produces superb 1080p video footage with virtually no moir
  • Image quality: Although only 12 megapixels, the a7S produces impressive image quality even at high ISO
  • S-Log2: Sony's excellent S-Log2 gamma profile preserves impressive dynamic range to facilitate color grading of video
  • Professional video tools: Adjustable zebras and focus peaking assist shooters in evaluating exposure and achieving sharp focus, particularly with third-party lenses

Things to consider

  • 4K video: The a7S produces class leading 4K (Ultra HD) video, but you'll need an external recorder to capture it.
  • Resolution: Despite having a full frame sensor, the a7S is limited to 12 megapixels for still photography
  • No touch screen: The a7S does not have a touch screen, a useful feature common on other hybrid still/video cameras
  • Battery: Battery life can be restrictive, especially when shooting video
Notable features
12MP CMOS sensor
The a7S' sensor may have only 12 million pixels, but this helps it excel in low light, while still allowing for wide, but not class-leading dynamic range. In addition, the low resolution allows for full sensor read-out, which means the a7S produces best-in-class video.
Tilting LCD
The a7S has the same 3" tilting LCD as its a7 siblings. The screen has 921k dots - average for this class - but sadly isn't touch-sensitive.
Electronic viewfinder
Photos and videos can also be composed on a large and beautiful 2.4 million dot OLED viewfinder.

The Low-light / Street / Video Beast
By spheremusic on July 11, 2014
Style Name: Base Verified Purchase
I had little interest in this camera when it was first announced, but after reading some of the reviews I decided to give it a chance. I was hesitant for the obvious reason: 12 MP. What is this, 2008? I understand why having fewer, larger pixels results in better high-ISO performance, but the previously reigning low-light king (according to DxOMark), the Nikon Df, has 16 MP, so 12 seemed a little stingy. As a street photographer who often shoots from the hip, literally, I like to have the option to crop, and 12 MP would leave me precious little margin for error.

First, the bad: Yes, 12 MP is limiting. People who tell you otherwise are either being disingenuous or only do photography where there's plenty of time to compose and adjust zoom or get closer as necessary. Any photography that demands capturing fleeting moments, including street and wedding, is going to suffer from the cropping constraints.

And yet, I still consider this the full-frame street photography king for two reasons: High ISO performance and the silent shutter option. I'm not interested in making night look like day, but the high-ISO performance allows me to use faster shutter speeds and greater depth of field so my subjects are neither blurry nor out of focus. I've never used any images above 6400 from the other cameras I've recently owned (D800, A7, E-M5), but with this camera I feel comfortable using 12800 and even 25600. People who say images are "usable" up to 51,200 probably have a different definition of usable than I do.
